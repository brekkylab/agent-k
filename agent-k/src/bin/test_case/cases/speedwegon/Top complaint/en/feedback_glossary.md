# Feedback Glossary

Purpose: define the different feedback sources so they don't get conflated.
Definitions only — this file holds no counts, no complaints, no survey data.

## Sources

- **Customer survey** — a structured in-product survey asking customers about
  their biggest frustration. Produces a ranked complaint tally.
- **Support tickets** — operational contacts when something breaks. Measures
  issues, not sentiment.
- **App-store reviews** — public, voluntary reviews. Skews to very happy or very
  frustrated.
- **NPS verbatims** — free-text comments attached to a 0–10 recommend score.
  Measures loyalty themes.
- **Exit interviews** — calls with customers who cancelled. Captures reasons for
  leaving.

## Why keep them separate

Each source answers a different question and usually has a different "top" item:

- Survey → most common complaint about the product.
- Tickets → most frequent operational issue.
- Reviews → loudest public gripe.
- NPS → leading loyalty detractor theme.
- Exit → leading reason for churn.

Reporting one source's top item as another's is a common mistake. When asked for
the most common SURVEY complaint, use the survey results — not tickets, reviews,
NPS, or exit interviews.

## Related metrics

- **CSAT** — satisfaction with a specific interaction.
- **NRR** — net revenue retention.
- **Activation** — reaching first value.

## Reminder

This glossary defines the sources; it never contains their data. The actual
most-common survey complaint lives in the customer survey results file.
