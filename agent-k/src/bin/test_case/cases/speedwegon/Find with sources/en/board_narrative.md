# Board Narrative — FY2025 Review (Talking Points)

Prepared for: board meeting
Author: Olive Park

These are narrative talking points. They describe trends in words and
deliberately avoid restating exact figures — for the audited numbers, point the
board to the FY2025 annual report.

## The story of the year

Last year was about resilience. We grew revenue at a healthy double-digit rate
while absorbing real SMB churn pressure. The growth was broad: every segment
contributed, and enterprise quietly became our highest-margin business.

## What went well

- Revenue grew solidly year over year (see annual report for the exact figure).
- Gross margin held in the low seventies.
- Enterprise expansion outpaced plan.

## What was hard

- SMB renewals shortened and churn rose.
- Pricing clarity remained a recurring complaint.
- Onboarding length was the single most cited friction.

## Strategic priorities going in

- Fix SMB onboarding with a proof-led flow.
- Keep enterprise momentum without starving SMB.
- Hold pricing; fix the explanation.

## What we are asking the board

- Endorse the FY2026 plan (targets in the budget document).
- Approve the onboarding investment.

## Note on numbers

This narrative intentionally states no revenue figure. When a precise number is
needed — including last year's total revenue — cite the FY2025 annual report,
which carries the audited actuals.

## Closing

The thesis holds: land SMB, grow mid-market, expand enterprise. Execution on
onboarding is the unlock for the year ahead.
