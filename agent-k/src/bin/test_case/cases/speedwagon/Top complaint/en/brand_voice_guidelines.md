# Brand Voice Guidelines

KlientCo's voice is plain, warm, and confident. This is a style reference and
holds no customer feedback or survey data.

## Principles

1. Lead with the customer's problem, not our feature.
2. Be specific; concrete nouns beat adjectives.
3. Respect the reader's time.
4. Be honest about limits.

## Do

- Use "you" and "your," not "users."
- Prefer verbs over nominalizations.
- Show proof: a number, a quote, a before/after.
- Read it aloud; if you stumble, cut it.

## Don't

- Avoid jargon: "synergy," "leverage," "best-in-class," "robust."
- Don't over-promise or use superlatives we can't back up.
- Don't blame the reader in support copy.

## Tone by surface

- Onboarding: encouraging, step-by-step.
- Support: calm, direct, no blame.
- Marketing: proof-led and specific.

## Words we like

clear, simple, proof, show, help, ready, plain, fast

## Words we avoid

revolutionary, seamless, frictionless, game-changing, world-class

## Formatting

- Sentence case for headings.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Lists for steps; prose for reasoning.

## Examples

- Weak: "Our robust platform leverages synergy to delight users."
- Better: "KlientCo shows your team what changed this week, in one screen."

## Reminder

This document is about how we write, not what customers complain about. Survey
complaints are tracked in the survey results file, not here.
