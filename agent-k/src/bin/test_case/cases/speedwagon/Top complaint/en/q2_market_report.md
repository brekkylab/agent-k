# Q2 Market Report

Prepared by: Market Research

Market-level analysis for the quarter. This is industry context, not customer
feedback, and contains no survey results.

## Summary

The SMB renewal cycle shortened materially this quarter, shifting weight to
onboarding over sales. Enterprise demand is steady but slow to convert, gated by
perceived integration effort rather than price.

## SMB

- Faster decisions, faster churn.
- Proof-led messaging beats feature-led in tests.
- Onboarding length is the recurring friction in the category.

## Enterprise

- Healthy pipeline, slow conversion.
- Security and integration questions dominate late stages.
- Governance narratives resonate at this tier.

## Competitive landscape

- Usage-based vendors gaining in mid-market.
- Seat-minimum pricing reads as "enterprise-only" to small buyers.

## Implications for us

- Invest in onboarding to defend SMB.
- Build integration proof for enterprise.
- Hold pricing; sharpen the explanation.

## Risks

- SMB net revenue keeps sliding if onboarding is not fixed.
- Enterprise deals slip without integration proof.

## Method

- Blends third-party reports with our own win/loss notes.
- Directional, not a precise market model.

## Reminder

This is market analysis. It is not the customer survey and holds no complaint
