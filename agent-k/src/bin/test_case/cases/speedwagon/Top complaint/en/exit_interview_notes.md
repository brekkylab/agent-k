# Churn Exit Interview Notes (Q2)

Source: calls with customers who cancelled, Q2
Interviews: 18

These notes capture why customers LEFT. That is a different question from "the
most common complaint in the survey." The leading churn reason below should not
be reported as the survey's top complaint.

## Stated reasons for leaving

| reason | count |
|--------|-------|
| Switched to a cheaper tool | 7 |
| Never fully onboarded | 5 |
| Lacked a required integration | 4 |
| Internal project cancelled | 2 |

The leading churn reason is **"Switched to a cheaper tool" (7 of 18)**.

## Observations

- Price-driven churn concentrated in solo and very small teams.
- "Never fully onboarded" accounts rarely reached first value.
- Integration gaps lost a few mid-market accounts.

## Quotes

- "It worked, but we found something cheaper that was good enough."
- "We signed up and never really got going."
- "Needed a connector you didn't have."

## What we're doing

- Proof-led onboarding to fix the "never onboarded" group.
- Integration roadmap prioritized by lost-deal reasons.

## Reminder

Exit interviews explain departures, not survey sentiment. The survey's most
common complaint is recorded in the customer survey results file, separately.

## Limits

- Small sample (18); treat as directional.
- Self-reported reasons may understate product gaps.
