# Project Glossary

A shared vocabulary so docs and meetings mean the same thing. Definitions only —
no dates, figures, or schedules live here.

## Segments

- **SMB** — small and mid-sized businesses; self-serve heavy, price-sensitive.
- **Mid-market** — between SMB and enterprise; some procurement, faster than ENT.
- **Enterprise (ENT)** — large accounts; security review and procurement required.

## Metrics

- **Net revenue retention (NRR)** — revenue from existing customers including
  expansion and churn, as a percentage of the prior period.
- **Churn** — customers (or revenue) lost in a period.
- **Time-to-decision** — days from first touch to a buy/no-buy decision.
- **Activation** — the point a new user reaches first meaningful value.

## Go-to-market

- **ICP** — ideal customer profile; who we build and sell for.
- **Proof-led onboarding** — onboarding that leads with evidence, not features.
- **Soft launch** — limited release to one segment before general availability.
- **Public launch** — general availability to all segments.

## Product

- **Dashboard** — the analytics surface customers see.
- **Data export** — pushing customer data out to other tools.
- **Integration effort** — perceived work to connect us to a customer's stack.

## Roles

- **Launch owner** — accountable for the launch plan and its dates.
- **Vendor owner** — manages a third-party vendor relationship.
- **DRI** — directly responsible individual for a given item.

## Statuses

- **Green** — on track.
- **Amber** — at risk, needs attention.
- **Red** — blocked or off track.

## Notes on usage

When a term implies a date (like "soft launch"), do not assume the date — look
it up in the schedule document. This glossary defines meaning, not timing.
