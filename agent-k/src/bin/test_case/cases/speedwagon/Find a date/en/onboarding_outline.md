# Onboarding Outline (Draft)

Owner: Owen Mathers
Status: in review

A proof-led onboarding flow for new SMB users. Goal: reach first value fast and
make the value visible. This is a content/flow outline — it sets no dates and is
not a schedule.

## Principles

- Show value before asking for setup.
- One action per step; never a wall of fields.
- Prove it with the user's own data as soon as possible.

## Step 1 — Welcome

- One line on what they'll get, not what we are.
- A single primary action: "Connect your data."
- Skip option that still leads somewhere useful.

## Step 2 — Connect data

- Offer the two most common sources first.
- If they skip, load a sample dataset so the next step still works.
- Confirm with a tiny success state, not a modal.

## Step 3 — First insight

- Show one chart built from their (or sample) data.
- Caption it in plain language: what changed and why it matters.
- This is the "aha" — keep it on screen, no interruptions.

## Step 4 — Invite a teammate

- Optional, low-pressure.
- Pre-fill a short message they can edit.

## Step 5 — Next best action

- Suggest exactly one follow-up based on what they connected.
- Link to a 90-second explainer, not a manual.

## Copy notes

- Proof-led wording throughout (see brand voice guidelines).
- Avoid "users," prefer "you."
- No countdowns, no fake urgency.

## Open questions

- Should Step 2 support a third data source at launch? (owner: Milo)
- Do we need a separate enterprise variant? (probably later)

## Out of scope

Timing and rollout belong to the launch schedule, not this outline. Do not infer
any launch date from this document.
