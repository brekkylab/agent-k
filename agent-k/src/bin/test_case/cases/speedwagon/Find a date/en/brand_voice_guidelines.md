# Brand Voice Guidelines

KlientCo's voice is plain, warm, and confident. We explain before we sell, and
we never make the reader feel behind. This document has no dates — it is style
guidance only.

## Principles

1. Lead with the customer's problem, not our feature.
2. Be specific. Concrete nouns beat adjectives.
3. Respect the reader's time. Short sentences, short paragraphs.
4. Be honest about limits. Trust compounds.

## Do

- Use "you" and "your," not "users."
- Prefer verbs over nominalizations ("decide," not "make a decision").
- Show proof: a number, a quote, a before/after.
- Read it aloud. If you stumble, cut it.

## Don't

- Avoid jargon and buzzwords: "synergy," "leverage," "best-in-class," "robust."
- Don't over-promise or use superlatives we can't back up.
- Don't bury the point under throat-clearing intros.
- Don't blame the reader in support copy.

## Tone by surface

- Onboarding: encouraging, step-by-step, forgiving of mistakes.
- Support: calm, direct, no blame, always a next step.
- Marketing: proof-led and specific, never hype.
- Sales follow-up: helpful, low-pressure, concrete.

## Words we like

clear, simple, proof, show, help, ready, plain, fast

## Words we avoid

revolutionary, seamless, frictionless, game-changing, world-class

## Formatting

- Sentence case for headings.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Lists for steps; prose for reasoning.
- Link to sources rather than restating them.

## Examples

- Weak: "Our robust platform leverages synergy to delight users."
- Better: "KlientCo shows your team what changed this week, in one screen."

When in doubt, choose the plainer phrasing. Clarity is the brand.
