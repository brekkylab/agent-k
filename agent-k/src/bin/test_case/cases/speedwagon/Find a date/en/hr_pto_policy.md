# HR — Paid Time Off Policy

Applies to: all full-time employees
Policy owner: People Operations

This is an internal HR policy. It contains no project, contract, or launch
dates. Any calendar references here are about leave accrual rules, not delivery.

## Accrual

- Full-time employees accrue 1.67 days of PTO per month (20 days per year).
- Accrual begins on your start date and is prorated for partial months.
- Unused PTO rolls over up to a cap of 10 days into the next year.
- Rollover above the cap is forfeited at year end.

## Requesting time off

1. Submit the request in the HR tool at least 5 business days in advance.
2. Your manager approves or declines within 2 business days.
3. For 5+ consecutive days, give 3 weeks' notice where possible.

## Sick leave

- Sick leave is separate from PTO: 10 days per year, no rollover.
- No advance notice required; notify your manager as early as you can.
- A doctor's note may be requested for 3+ consecutive sick days.

## Holidays

- The company observes 11 public holidays.
- Two floating holidays may be used for any day of personal significance.

## Parental leave

- Up to 16 weeks of paid parental leave.
- Coordinate timing with People Ops and your manager.

## Carryover and payout

- On departure, accrued unused PTO is paid out.
- Sick leave is not paid out on departure.

## Approvals during busy periods

Managers may limit overlapping time off during heavy workloads, but cannot deny
legally protected leave. Plan around team coverage, not around product dates.

## Questions

Contact People Operations. This document governs leave only and should never be
used as a source for project scheduling.
