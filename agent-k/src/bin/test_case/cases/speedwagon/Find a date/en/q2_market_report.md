# Q2 Market Report — SMB & Enterprise

Prepared by: Market Research
Reporting period: Q2 (April–June)

## Summary

The SMB renewal cycle shortened by roughly 18% quarter over quarter. Buyers are
deciding faster and churning faster, which puts the weight on onboarding rather
than on the sales motion. Enterprise demand is steady but slow to convert, and
the dominant blocker is perceived integration effort — not price.

## SMB segment

- Renewal windows compressed; the median time-to-decision fell from 21 to 17 days.
- Proof-led onboarding language outperformed feature-led messaging in every test.
- The top stated reason for churn was "onboarding took too long."
- Second was "pricing felt unclear," which is a messaging problem, not a price one.

Recommended focus: a short proof asset embedded in the first onboarding step,
and a clearer pricing explainer. Neither requires a pricing change.

## Enterprise segment

- Pipeline is healthy but conversion lags by about a quarter.
- Security and integration questions dominate late-stage deals.
- A governance narrative resonates more than a feature list at this tier.

Recommended focus: an integration-effort one-pager and a security overview.

## Competitive notes

Usage-based vendors are gaining in mid-market. Seat-minimum pricing is read as
"enterprise-only" by smaller buyers and quietly removes us from SMB shortlists.

## Risks

- If onboarding friction is not addressed, SMB net revenue keeps sliding.
- Enterprise deals may slip a quarter without better integration proof.

## Takeaways

1. Prioritize SMB retention messaging this half.
2. Build one proof asset for onboarding.
3. Revisit enterprise positioning around integrations.
4. Hold pricing steady; fix the explanation instead.

No pricing changes are recommended this quarter. This report is market analysis
only and does not set any launch, contract, or delivery dates.
